faking productivity

it's finals season, and here i see bodies all over the floor. students sleeping in study rooms, with half-finished snacsk all around.

i remember when i used to be like this. faking prodcutivity. faking sacrifice. telling yourself you did all you could, even though in reality you've procrasitanted until now.

this behavior is a self-defence mechanism. it's a last ditch resort, and the last play in your playbook you can use to spare your ego the misery of the consequences of your actions.

pulling all nighters, not sleeping for days. it seems rough. and it is. but it's definitely not how things get done in the real world.

sleeping on the floor of your study lounge, pulling all nighters is nothing but an excuse for mediocrity. a brief mirage of determination. determination that only shows up to guard you against your own mediocrity.

be consistent. get good sleep. do deep work. it's that simple.

good results, succesful projects, and good relationships are built over time, and then all at once.
