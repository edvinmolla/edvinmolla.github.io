however, modern engineering has managed to reliably produce enormous ships that enable the global supply chain system, and they must be capable of navigating mother nature's inherent dangers.

much like these ships, we tend to have a direction in life as well. life's waves and currents will often throw us off course, but the more momentum and inertia we have, the harder it will be for us to completely change direction. i like to think of momentum as demonstrated competence coupled with unwaverign dedication toward a certain goal. no matter what happens in life â€“ other than death, it will be difficult to throw a dedicated human off course.

by remaining vigilent and aware, one can maintain course and engine power, all the while waiting for the storm to pass and the waves to calm, and eventually continue in the desired course. hence, patience seems to be a virtue that pays off over the long term.
